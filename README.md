ShopSphere E-Commerce Analysis
ShopSphere is a US-based e-commerce company that sells popular consumer electronics and accessories. Established in 2018, the company has grown and expanded in the last few years, it has encountered increasing competition from peer companies as well as unique challenges and opportunities.

ShopSphere's book of business in 2021-2022 is approaching 3998 customers and possesses over 5885 transactions, generating successful transaction revenue exceeding $1 billion. The available eCommerce data spans various dimensions and metrics, including sales, products, transaction status, and payment method. Reporting and Answering questions the Marketing and Finance Team, an in-depth analysis was conducted to evaluate ShopSphere's performance over the past several years (2021–2022). This comprehensive review provides valuable insights that internal cross-functional teams will utilize to streamline processes and enhance ShopSphere's commercial performance. The key insights and recommendations focus on the following areas:

Sales trends - Focusing on key metrics of sales revenue, number of orders placed, and average order value (AOV).
Product performance - Analyzing different product lines, payment method and transaction status.
Customer berhaviour analysis - Evaluating the the different behaviour of customers and providing recommendations to maximize customer engagement.

Customer Behaviour
 
With 1984 customers with normal purchase (Register first then order) and 880 Pre-Register customers (Order first then Register), Customer segmentation is designed to reflect intrinsic purchasing behavior. Product attributes such as brand and category are analyzed post-clustering to interpret and activate each segment.


Revenue growth is driven by episodic high-value events rather than sustained customer activity. Loyal normal customers are declining, while pre-registered customers show high potential but inconsistent engagement. This indicates strong upside with weak retention fundamentals.
Normal Customer

The Loyal high-value customer activity starts mid-2021, peaks around September 2021 / January 2022 with total revenue $716.79M. Units and revenue move together, after mid-2022, both collapse toward near-zero.
Normal but low engagement segment has very low activity early 2021 then a sharp growth mid-2021 with $27,883M in revenue. Several spiky peaks in both units and revenue, and the revenue itself sometimes exceeds unit growth. This means the purchases are campaign-driven, not habitual.
Pre-register

Extreme high volume segment has one very sharp revenue spike in April 2022 with $237,130,190 in revenue. Likely bulk or wholesale-type behavior. That spike is probably one large order, one special event, or one major customer.
Loyal high value has almost flat activity for a long time. Then one massive revenue spike with minimal unit increase, $570,775,388 in revenue.
Normal but low engagement shows us that pre-registration does not guarantee activation. Behavior mirrors low-engagement normal customers.

Normal customers contribute most of the transaction volume through repeated low-engagement purchases, providing operational stability. Pre-registered customers, while fewer in volume, are more likely to generate high-impact purchases and bulk behavior. This creates a trade-off between stability and upside that requires different engagement strategies per segment.

Recommendations
1. Reduce reliance on anomaly-driven revenue by stabilizing core product performance
Revenue in 2022 was heavily driven by one-off events (e.g., September Mobile & Tablets spike), which are not sustainable. ShopSphere should prioritize consistently performing categories such as Appliances, which show balanced revenue and unit sales, and reduce over-dependence on short-term product surges.

2. Refine pricing, cost control, and promotions to improve profit consistency
The revenue is volume-driven while profit is event-driven, indicating pricing and cost inefficiencies. ShopSphere should renegotiate supplier pricing, limit aggressive discounts during high-volume periods, and focus promotions on improving margin, not just order count.

3. Rebuild customer retention with segment-specific engagement strategies
Loyal normal customers declined sharply after mid-2022, while pre-registered customers showed high-value but inconsistent behavior. Implement loyalty incentives for repeat normal customers and targeted activation campaigns for high-potential pre-registered customers to balance stability and upside.

4. Leverage high-performing brands for bundled and upsell strategies
Samsung and Apple consistently generate the highest revenue and AOV. ShopSphere should use these brands as anchor products for bundles, cross-sells, and premium offers to lift AOV across lower-impact categories such as Superstore and Books.
